Dermo Peel is an advanced, acid-free skin resurfacing treatment that gently renews the skin without traditional chemical acids. Using natural freshwater sponge spicules, botanical extracts and enzymes, it stimulates the skin's natural renewal process — helping to improve texture, scars, dullness, uneven skin tone, mild pigmentation, congestion, enlarged pores and the appearance of fine lines.

Unlike many resurfacing treatments, Dermo Peel encourages healthy cell turnover while supporting the skin barrier, making it an excellent option for visible results with minimal downtime. It's also an ideal preparation treatment before more intensive corrective procedures such as Bio Spicules, Nano Infusion or SSR Skin Rejuvenation.

Dermo Peel combines a unique blend of natural ingredients that resurface and strengthen the skin:

  • Freshwater sponge spicules — gentle mechanical exfoliation while stimulating renewal and collagen production
  • Pineapple enzymes (Bromelain) — dissolve dead skin cells to brighten dull skin without harsh acids
  • Aloe Vera — soothes, hydrates and supports healing
  • Horsetail Extract — rich in silica to strengthen skin and support collagen
  • Peppermint Extract — refreshes with a cooling sensation
  • Marine Plankton Extract — improves skin vitality, hydration and repair

The treatment is completely acid-free — an excellent alternative for clients who cannot tolerate traditional chemical peels.

Most clients experience very little downtime. Immediately after treatment, the skin may appear slightly pink or flushed, similar to mild sun exposure. A light prickly or tingling sensation is common for up to 24–48 hours as the natural spicules continue stimulating the skin.

Over the following 2–3 days you may notice:

  • Mild dryness or tightness
  • Light flaking or peeling
  • Skin feeling slightly rough as old cells shed
  • Brighter, smoother, fresher-looking skin as new skin is revealed
A series of 4–6 treatments spaced 2–3 weeks apart is recommended. Improvement is often visible after a single session.
Not suitable for clients with active rosacea, highly inflamed or broken skin, those taking or recently completing Roaccutane, those using skin-thinning medications, or clients with a known crustacean or shellfish allergy.

Microneedling, also known as Collagen Induction Therapy (CIT), is an advanced skin rejuvenation treatment that uses fine, sterile needles to create controlled micro-channels in the skin. This stimulates your body's natural healing response, encouraging collagen and elastin production to improve skin texture, firmness and overall skin health.

Microneedling can help improve:

  • Acne scars and other atrophic scars
  • Fine lines and wrinkles
  • Dull, tired-looking skin
  • Loss of firmness and elasticity
  • Mild stretch marks
Most clients experience redness similar to mild sunburn for 24–48 hours. Mild swelling, tightness and light flaking may occur over the following few days as your skin renews. Most people return to normal daily activities the following day while following the recommended aftercare.
Most clients achieve the best results with a course of 3–6 treatments, spaced 4–6 weeks apart. More advanced scarring or skin concerns may require additional sessions. Your personalised treatment plan will be discussed during your consultation.

Microneedling may not be suitable if you:

  • Are pregnant or breastfeeding
  • Have active acne, skin infections or open wounds
  • Have active cold sores in the treatment area
  • Are taking Roaccutane or have used it within the past 6–12 months
  • Have certain autoimmune or medical conditions that affect healing
  • Have a history of keloid scarring
  • Have uncontrolled diabetes or poor wound healing

A thorough consultation will determine whether microneedling is right for you.
